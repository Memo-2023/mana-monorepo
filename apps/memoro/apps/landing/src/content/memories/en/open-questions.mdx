# Open Questions

The Open Questions Memory identifies all unresolved points and clarification needs from your conversation.

## Key Features

### Automatic Recognition
Identifies different types of open points:
- Explicitly unanswered questions
- Deferred decisions
- Missing information
- Unclear responsibilities
- Technical clarification needs
- Strategic uncertainties

### Priority Classification
Each open question is ranked by urgency:
- **Critical**: Blocks further work
- **Important**: Should be clarified soon
- **Nice-to-know**: Can be clarified later

### Solution Suggestions
For each question, suggestions are provided:
- Possible contacts
- Information sources
- Next steps for clarification
- Estimated effort
- Deadline for clarification

## Use Cases

- Preparation for follow-up meetings
- Identification of knowledge gaps
- Risk management in projects
- Decision preparation
- Escalation of critical points
- Define research tasks

## Output Format

### Sorted by Priority

**Critical Open Questions**

**Question:** **What budget is available for Q4?**
- *Context*: Project planning depends on it
- *Clarification by*: CFO Roland Stutz
- *Next step*: Schedule budget meeting
- *Deadline*: By Aug 15, 2025

**Question:** **Are the new privacy policies compatible with our architecture?**

**Nice-to-know Questions**

## Tips for Optimal Results

1. **Explicitly mark questions**
   - "This needs to be clarified"
   - "The question remains open"
   - "I don't have an answer to that"

2. **Provide context**
   - Why is the question important?
   - What depends on the answer?
   - Who is affected?

3. **Name responsibilities**
   - "Only X can answer that"
   - "Y is responsible for that"
   - "We need to clarify with Z"

4. **Define timeframe**
   - "We need this by..."
   - "At the latest before..."
   - "Critical for the launch"
